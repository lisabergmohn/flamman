Discover Freedom

The Right of Public Access, or "allemansrätten" in Sweden, grants everyone the freedom to explore nature—hiking, camping, and cycling—without needing landowners' permission. This unique principle emphasizes respect for nature and responsible recreation, requiring visitors to leave no trace and avoid disturbing wildlife. It fosters a deep connection with nature, promoting physical and mental well-being while balancing freedom with responsibility. Inform yourself further by clicking "Read more" (pdf).

Staying safe

In Sweden, outdoor activities often involve making fires. Choose safe spots, like gravel or sandy ground, away from dry vegetation, and use designated fire pits or portable stoves when possible. Surround the fire with stones or a trench to contain it. Use only loose branches and twigs found on the ground, avoiding live or standing dead trees. Never make fires in windy conditions, and always have water ready to fully extinguish the fire and any remaining embers. Inform yourself further with safety guidelines by clicking "Read more" (pdf).
